Essential Experiences

Canal Cruise

Explore Amsterdam's iconic canals by taking a leisurely cruise. Admire the picturesque bridges, historic buildings, and charming houseboats while learning about the city's rich history

Indulge in Dutch Cuisine

Sample traditional Dutch delicacies such as stroopwafels, bitterballen, and herring from local markets and eateries. Don't forget to try Dutch cheese at the famous Cheese Museum Amsterdam.

Experience Nightlife

Experience Amsterdam's vibrant nightlife scene by exploring its bustling bars, cafes, and clubs. From laid-back pubs to chic cocktail bars, there's something for every taste and preference.

Tips for First-Time Visitors

  • Pre-purchase tickets where possible

      If you're visiting museums and galleries, expect long lines, especially from June to August. Make sure to book tickets early, at least six weeks in advance, for popular spots like the Anne Frank House and Van Gogh Museum.

  • Budget for a tourist tax

      In 2024, Amsterdam's tourist tax went up from 7% to 12.5%, making it the most expensive in Europe. Remember to budget for this tax when planning your trip, as it's easy to forget once you've booked everything.

  • Beware of cyclists

      In Amsterdam, bikes rule the roads, with the city being famously bike-friendly. Watch out for the red bike lanes, marked with bicycle icons, as many tourists mistakenly walk on them, posing potential safety risks.

  • Always bring your ID with you

      You'll need a photo ID for museum visits and may be asked for identification by police, especially if you seem intoxicated. To prevent any issues, always carry your ID; consider bringing your driver's license instead of your passport if you're concerned about losing it.
